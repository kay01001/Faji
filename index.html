📘 Beginner's guide to buying crypto

1 Choose a reliable exchange

Not all exchanges are equal. Look for platforms with strong security, low fees, and good liquidity. Here are our top picks for 2026:

ExchangeFeesCoinsSecurity
Binance0.1%600+Excellent
Coinbase0.5%250+Very good
Kraken0.16%200+Excellent

💡 Pro tip: For beginners, Coinbase is easiest; for low fees, Binance is best.

2 Create & verify your account

Sign up with email, enable 2FA, and complete KYC (ID verification). This usually takes 5‑10 minutes and is required by law.

3 Deposit funds

You can deposit via bank transfer (cheaper, slower) or credit/debit card (instant, higher fees). Minimum deposits vary: typically $10‑$50.

Always double-check deposit addresses — crypto transactions are irreversible.

4 Buy your first crypto

Navigate to the "Buy" section, select your coin (Bitcoin BTC, Ethereum ETH, etc.), enter amount, and confirm. The crypto will appear in your exchange wallet.

5 Transfer to a private wallet (important!)

For long‑term holding, move your coins to a wallet you control — like MetaMask (for Ethereum) or Trust Wallet (multi‑chain). This protects you from exchange hacks.

When copying wallet addresses, always verify the first and last 4 characters.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to buy crypto?

Yes, if you use reputable exchanges and secure your private keys. Always enable 2FA.

What is the minimum amount?

Most exchanges allow as little as $10. Some even have $1 buys.

Do I need to pay taxes?

In most countries, crypto is taxable. Consult a local expert.
