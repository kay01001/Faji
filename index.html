📘 Step‑by‑Step Guide

1. Choose an exchange

We recommend Binance, Coinbase, or Kraken. Compare fees, security, and available coins. Always use regulated platforms.

2. Deposit funds

Link your bank account or card. Bank transfers are cheaper (1‑3 days), cards are instant but have higher fees. Minimum deposits vary.

3. Buy crypto

Select a pair like BTC/USDT, enter amount, and execute. You can use market or limit orders. Your first purchase takes minutes.

4. Transfer to private wallet

For security, move coins to a wallet you control (e.g., MetaMask, Trust Wallet). Never share your seed phrase.

🔐 Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to keep crypto on an exchange?

Not recommended for long‑term storage. Exchanges can be hacked or freeze accounts. Use a hardware wallet for large amounts.

What is the minimum amount to start?

Most exchanges allow purchases from $10–$50. Some have no minimum for certain coins.

How do I avoid high fees?

Use bank transfers instead of cards, and consider using limit orders to reduce spread.
